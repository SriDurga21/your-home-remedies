Cinnamon Health Benefits

Cinnamon also called Dalchini, is a popular spice used in tea, desserts, and curries.

It contains powerful antioxidants and compounds like cinnamaldehyde, which provide many health benefits.

Helps Control Blood Sugar

Cinnamon may improve insulin sensitivity.

Helps regulate blood sugar levels, especially useful for people with diabetes.

Supports Heart Health

May help reduce bad cholesterol (LDL) and triglycerides.

Supports healthy blood circulation.

Rich in Antioxidants

Contains strong antioxidants that protect the body from cell damage and aging.

Anti-Inflammatory Properties

Helps reduce inflammation in the body.

May help with joint pain and arthritis.

Boosts Immunity

Has antibacterial and antifungal properties that help fight infections.

Improves Digestion

Helps relieve bloating, gas, and stomach discomfort.

May Help with Weight Management

Cinnamon may help boost metabolism and support fat burning.

Good for Brain Health

Some studies suggest cinnamon may help improve memory and brain function.

How to Use Cinnamon

Add 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon powder to warm water or tea.

Use in oatmeal, smoothies, or desserts.

Add a cinnamon stick while cooking biryani or curries.

Precautions

Use in moderate amounts.

Excess cinnamon may cause liver irritation due to coumarin content.
