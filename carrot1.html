Carrot Health Benefits

Carrot is a nutritious root vegetable rich in vitamins, minerals, fiber, and antioxidants.

It is especially known for its high beta-carotene content.

Good for Eye Health

Carrots contain beta-carotene, which the body converts into vitamin A.

Helps improve vision and supports eye health.

Boosts Immunity

Rich in vitamin C and antioxidants that help protect the body from infections.

Supports Heart Health

Fiber and potassium help reduce cholesterol and regulate blood pressure.

Good for Skin

Antioxidants help keep skin healthy and glowing.

May help slow signs of aging.

Helps in Weight Management

Low in calories and high in fiber, which helps you feel full longer.

Improves Digestion

Fiber supports healthy digestion and prevents constipation.

Supports Brain Health

Antioxidants help protect brain cells from damage.

May Reduce Risk of Some Diseases

Carrots contain compounds that may help reduce the risk of certain chronic diseases.

How to Use Carrots

Eat raw carrots in salads.

Drink fresh carrot juice.

Add to soups, curries, and stir-fried dishes.

Precautions

Excess carrot consumption may cause temporary yellowing of the skin due to high beta-carotene.
